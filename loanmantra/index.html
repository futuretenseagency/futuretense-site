01. WHO THEY ARE

2008

Built from a broken system.

2008

The gap Raj couldn't ignore

When the financial crisis hit, Raj Tulshan was nine years into Wall Street at Merrill Lynch's Principal Investments and Structured Products Group. He watched the commercial lending system fracture and saw exactly who got left behind: small business owners who had to visit ten different places just to understand their options. He left to fix it.

2013

Loan Mantra is founded

After completing his MBA at Oxford's Said Business School, Raj formally founded Loan Mantra in Charleston, SC. The thesis was simple: one secure platform where a small business owner could organize their financials, apply for any loan type, and connect with the right lender. High-tech. High-touch. No silos.

2019

Public launch, then a pandemic.

Loan Mantra launched publicly in March 2019. A year in, with momentum building and a major launch event planned for April 2020, COVID-19 shut the country down. Rather than retreat, the team pivoted immediately to helping small businesses navigate EIDL and PPP programs. The crisis became proof of concept.

2022

BLUE and CLUE go live

The proprietary platform splits into two products: BLUE for small business borrowers and CLUE for lenders. Both achieve NIST-800-53 v5 government-level cybersecurity compliance. Loan approval times drop by 30% or more for borrowers on the platform.

2024

Rebrand. New market. Emily joins.

Loan Mantra relaunches with a new brand, new website, and a sharpened focus on franchise financing. Emily Larsen Doxford joins as VP of Marketing, bringing 15 years across fintech, media, and nonprofits. The franchise push culminates at MUFC 2026 in Las Vegas, where Raj and Emily arrive ready to show the industry what franchise financing looks like when the technology actually works.

Return on one closed deal

A single funded franchise deal for Loan Mantra typically involves a loan of $500K to $5M. Even at a conservative advisory fee, one closed deal covers the entire program cost for multiple months. The goal in month one is to establish baseline CPL and conversion rate. If the program generates 80 leads per month and Loan Mantra closes even 2% of them, that is one to two new funded deals per month from a standing start. Every dollar of ad spend becomes more precise from there.
